Pioneer
MKII

An autonomous underwater vehicle where hydrodynamic form and FDM manufacturing constraints were resolved as one design problem — not in sequence.

Key decision
Torpedo–blowfish hybrid geometry reduces drag in forward motion while maintaining a flat reference face for FDM bed adhesion. Zero support material on the primary hull.

Pioneer: Form Decision
Pioneer MKII Form Decision
02 — Form strategy

Torpedo meets
blowfish

The nose tapers for hydrodynamic entry. The mid-section swells to maximise internal volume for electronics and ballast. This is not aesthetic — it is an engineering argument made visible in the outer surface.

FDM constraint resolved
The flat underside reference face means the entire hull prints without support material. One geometry solves hydrodynamics and manufacturability simultaneously.
HullFDM — PETG, 3-shell perimeter
Sections4 watertight compartments, O-ring sealed
AssemblyBayonet end caps, tool-free access

Rockhounder: Research
Rockhounder Research & Problem
02 — Research

Harness first,
bag second

Field observation with sport climbers revealed every existing pack was designed for hiking — then adapted for climbing. The harness is the primary wearable. The pack is secondary. The design process reversed this hierarchy.

Friction points identified
Hip belt vs. harness leg loops. Lid access while roped up. Gear loops blocked by shoulder straps. Each became a design criterion — not a trade-off.
UsersSport climbers, via ferrata, crag day trips
PrimaryWearable while harnessed and roped
AccessTop lid + front panel, single-handed

Delta
Delta
Convertible Furniture · Space-Constrained Users · Mechanism Design

Delta

Convertible furniture for space-constrained users, combining work and play in a single piece. A single telescopic hinge drives the full transition — no separate parts.

The problem
Users with limited living space need furniture that shifts between a work mode (desk + upright seat) and a play/leisure mode (reclined + footrest). Delta solves this with a single sliding joint — no additional hardware required.

Delta: Mechanism
Delta Mechanism Detail
02 — Single-hinge logic

One joint,
three states

A telescopic sliding connection allows simultaneous angular and linear displacement along a single axis — backrest angle and seat depth change together, driven by one motion, locking at three discrete positions.

Locking geometry
Red-tipped locking pins engage at three backrest angles. Pin geometry designed so that body weight tightens the lock — no spring-loaded release, no fatigue failure mode.
PositionsWork · Upright · Reclined
JointSingle telescopic sliding hinge
BodyPlywood / hardwood — no secondary mechanisms
Delta: Scale Prototype
Delta 1:5 Scale Prototype
03 — Verification

Mechanism
first

Before committing to full-scale fabrication, the mechanism was validated at 1:5 scale in FDM. The model confirmed all three locking positions were reachable and that the joint self-locked under simulated load.

What the model revealed
Pin clearance at the reclined position was too tight at 1:5 — identified before full-scale build and corrected in CAD. The scale model found the problem, not the final prototype.
Scale1:5 — all joint proportions maintained
MethodFDM — PLA, 0.15 mm layer height

Consumer Electronics · Reverse Engineering · DFM

Electric Razor
Redesign

A premium shaving adapter system that began with a teardown, not a brief. Before sketching, every competitor was disassembled, documented, and mapped — constraints defined before form explored.

Method
Competitive teardown first, then design. This reverses the typical process. The charging dock geometry was the last decision — not the first.
RoleSolo — research through production-ready model
ToolsRhino (model) · KeyShot (render)
Electric Razor: Sketch / Ideation
Electric Razor Redesign Sketch / Ideation
01 — Ideation

Sketching for
disassembly

Form alternatives annotated with the questions that drove the redesign: fewer parts, snap assembly, tool-free battery access, horizontal disassembly. The teardown findings are written directly onto the sketches.

Electric Razor: Teardown
Electric Razor Redesign Competitive Teardown
02 — Starting point

Disassemble
first

Before a single sketch, competing products were opened, measured, and mapped. The teardown revealed shared failure points: the adapter locking mechanism and charging contact arrangement — both addressed in Razor.

Key finding
All competitors used spring-friction locking. Razor uses a quarter-turn bayonet — positive engagement, single-handed operation, no spring fatigue failure mode.
MethodFull disassembly + dimensional mapping
FailuresLocking, charging contact, seal degradation
ResponseBayonet lock, magnetic charge, dual O-ring
